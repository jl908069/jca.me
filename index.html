Jessica Lin

Ph.D., Department of Linguistics, Georgetown University · yl1290 [at] georgetown [dot] edu

My name is Jessica (Yi-Ju) Lin (林宜儒). I recently completed my PhD in Computational Linguistics at Georgetown University (June 2025), where I’m advised by Amir Zeldes and a member of Corpling Lab and Computational Linguistics @ Georgetown (GUCL). Before coming to Georgetown, I recieved my M.A. in Linguistics from National Taiwan University and B.A. in Applied Chinese Language and Culture from National Taiwan Normal University. Check out my my CV here.

My dissertation, "Entity Salience in Discourse: A Computational and Corpus Approach", seeks to investigate entity salience in discourse from a computational and corpus approach. My dissertation committee consists of Amir Zeldes, Nathan Schneider, and Massimo Poesio.

Research Interests:

  • Entity Linking, Salient Entity Extraction
  • Hate Speech Detection, Multimodal Hate Speech Detection
  • Resources & Evaluation, Corpus Linguistics
  • Societal applications of NLP, NLP for Social Good
